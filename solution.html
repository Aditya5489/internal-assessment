Types of Plant

Plants are incredibly diverse and can be classified into several types based on their characteristics, habitats, and biological functions. Broadly, they are divided into two categories: vascular and non-vascular plants. Vascular plants, like trees, shrubs, and flowering plants, have specialized tissues (xylem and phloem) for water and nutrient transportation. Non-vascular plants, such as mosses and liverworts, lack these tissues and thrive in moist environments. Within vascular plants, there are angiosperms (flowering plants) and gymnosperms (non-flowering plants like conifers). Plants can also be categorized based on their lifecycle as annuals, biennials, and perennials, or by their habitat, such as aquatic plants, desert plants (succulents), and tropical plants. Each type of plant plays a vital role in maintaining ecological balance, providing oxygen, food, shelter, and contributing to the Earth's biodiversity.
